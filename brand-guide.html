01 — Brand

Who we are

South End creates small-group endurance adventures — trail running and cycling trips in the Alps and beyond. Hard, beautiful, and deliberately small. No coaches, no crowds.

Positioning: the trips we wanted to do and couldn't book anywhere. Promise: earn the view. Tagline: Earn the view.

02 — Logo

The mark

A minimal mountain peak. Use the full lockup where there's room; the mark alone for avatars, favicons and small spaces. Keep clear space around it equal to the height of the peak. Never stretch, recolour outside the palette, or add effects.

05 — Voice

How we sound

Confident, dry, minimal. Short sentences. Let the mountains do the shouting.

"Some trips you book. This one, you earn."

Do

  • Short, declarative sentences
  • Specifics — numbers, places, distances
  • Understatement over hype
  • Let white space and photos breathe

Avoid

  • "Epic", "amazing", "bucket list", "once in a lifetime"
  • Exclamation marks
  • Corporate tour-operator speak
  • Over-explaining — say less
06 — Photography

Imagery

Big, full-bleed, slightly desaturated. Mountains, movement, scale. People small in the landscape, not posed. Often moving — slow pans or video. The image is the hero; type sits quietly on top.
